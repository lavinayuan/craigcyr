* Read legal requirements for Ekol Dicle, blank guns .

Legal requirements for Ekol Dicle, blank guns:

  • In Canada these guns are some of the few blank guns available for purchase and or rental that are not prohibited by Canadian law and do not require a permit to carry, or the presence of ETF on closed sets. However, in some cases ETF will be required on open sets where there is public access, noise by-laws, etc.
  • These guns are made with meticulous realistic construction with similar weight. Great for film or training purposes as they operate exactly like real guns without deadly force. These guns will not operate with any real ammunition and cannot be modified in any way to fire real ammunition as the manufacturer has built in countermeasures for total failure of the firearm if tampered with.

Question 4 from the RCMP website Canadian Firearms Program Guide:
"What is considered to be a firearm for the purposes of the Firearms Act and for offences related to the Firearms Act in the Criminal code?"

A4. As set out in the Firearms Act, "firearm" means:

  • a barrelled weapon from which any shot, bullet or other projectile can be discharged and that is capable of causing serious bodily injury or death to a person, and includes
    • any frame or receiver of such a barrelled weapon, as well as
    • anything that can be adapted for use as a firearm.
  • Devices designed exclusively for signalling, for notifying of distress, for firing blank cartridges or for firing stud cartridges, explosive-driven rivets or other industrial projectiles;
  • Shooting devices designed exclusively forslaughtering domestic animals, tranquilizing animals, or discharging projectiles with lines attached to them;
  • Air Guns and other barreled weapons designed to have:
    • A muzzle velocity of 152.4 meters per second or less and/or
    • A muzzle energy of 5.7 joules or less.
